Decentralized Bank

A decentralized bank app, often referred to as a decentralized finance (DeFi) application, is a financial application built on blockchain technology. Unlike traditional banks, which are centralized and rely on intermediaries like banks and financial institutions, decentralized bank apps operate on open and decentralized blockchain networks.

Banking Services

Deposit:

Create a user-friendly interface where users can log in or connect their wallets using Web3 or a similar technology. Provide a "Deposit" button or form where users can enter the amount of cryptocurrency they want to deposit. Use a smart contract to handle the deposit transaction. The smart contract should record the user's deposit in a decentralized ledger. Ensure that the deposited funds are converted into the appropriate cryptocurrency format (e.g., wei for Ethereum) and stored securely in the user's account.

Withdraw:

Offer a "Withdraw" button or form in the user interface. Prompt users to enter the withdrawal amount and select the cryptocurrency they wish to withdraw (if applicable). Use the smart contract to process the withdrawal request. Verify that the user has sufficient funds in their account to cover the withdrawal. Execute the withdrawal by transferring the requested cryptocurrency to the user's external wallet address

Account Balances:

Display the user's account balance prominently on their dashboard after they log in. Ensure that the account balance is updated in real-time to reflect recent deposits, withdrawals, or interest accruals. Provide a "Refresh" or "Sync" button to manually update the balance if needed.
